Uzbekistan

Uzbekistan (officially: Republic of Uzbekistan) is a state in Central Central Asia. The capital of Uzbekistan is Tashkent, and the official language is Uzbek. Area - 448 978 [4] km2. Population (2020) - 34 011 621 [5]. Currency is the som. The Republic of Uzbekistan consists of 12 regions, the city of Tashkent and the Republic of Karakalpakstan and is an independent, democratic, secular and legal State. Uzbekistan is a member of the CIS, the UN, the OSCE and the SCO. Uzbekistan has five landlocked countries: Kazakhstan to the north; Kyrgyzstan to the northeast; Tajikistan to the southeast; and Afghanistan to the south; and it borders Turkmenistan in the southwest. Uzbekistan's economy is gradually moving towards a market economy, and its foreign trade policy is based on import substitution. In September 2017, the country's currency is fully converted at the market rate. Uzbekistan is a major producer and exporter of cotton fiber. The country is also home to the world's largest gold mine. Uzbekistan has become the largest electricity producer in Central Asia, with huge Soviet-era power generation capacity and natural gas supplies.

Origin of the word Uzbek

There are several hypotheses about the origin of the Uzbek ethnonym. The first mention of the Uzbek word as a personal name dates back to the XII century. The word Uzbek originated among the Turkic tribes in Movarounnahr before the arrival of the Mongols in the 13th century. The 12th-century Arab historian Osama ibn Munqiz, in his Book of Enlightenment, states that one of the leaders of the Seljuk period (1115-1116) was the ruler of Mosul, Emir Uzbek. According to the historian Rashid al-Din, the last representative of the Ildegiz dynasty who ruled in Tabriz was Uzbek Muzaffar (1210-1225) [7]. [8] Historian Mutal Irmatov suggested that the Uzbek word comes from the name "Ozlar" [9] Doctor of Historical Sciences, Professor R. G. Mukminova provided additional evidence against linking the ethnic name of the Uzbeks with the name of the Uzbek khan. Sharofiddin Ali Yazdi reports on the events of 1289, long before the reign of the Uzbek khan, and mentions the raid of Uzbek troops in Tabriz. [10] According to other historians, in the 1960s, a union of nomadic Turkic tribes called Uzbeks was formed in the Dashti-Kipchak region. In the 1960s, the ethnonym "Uzbek" became the collective name of the entire Turkic population of eastern Dashti-Kipchak. The ethnonym "Uzbek" came to Movarounnahr during the reign of Amir Temur. The great poet Alisher Navoi (1441-1501) mentioned the ethnonym "Uzbek" in Khorasan and Movarounnahr.

Culture

Cultural and social relations in Uzbekistan have a long history. Among the mass media (more than 1300) are the National TV and Radio Company of Uzbekistan, regional TV and radio companies, FM radio stations, state newspapers, as well as Internet resources. Thanks to the transition to digital technologies, the TV and radio industry in Uzbekistan has developed rapidly.

State flag of the Republic of Uzbekistan

The state flag is approved by the Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan of November 18, 1991 No. 407XII "On the State Flag of the Republic of Uzbekistan". The National Flag of the Republic of Uzbekistan is a rectangular cloth consisting of three stripes of blue, white and dark green colors along the entire length of the flag. The national flag of the Republic of Uzbekistan has a length of 250 centimeters and a width of 125 centimeters. The width of the blue, white, and green colors is the same. Each is 40 inches wide. In the center of the national flag of the Republic of Uzbekistan - red borders with a width of 2.5 centimeters. On the obverse and reverse sides of the blue flag in the upper part of the National Flag of the Republic of Uzbekistan, at the pole, there is a white new moon and next to it twelve white five-pointed stars. The image of a new white moon and twelve white five-pointed stars is located in a rectangle 70x30 cm in the middle of the blue top. The white new moon is in a vertical position with the side of the crest facing the pole, 20 cm away from the pole, and can fit in a circle with a diameter of 30 cm. Twelve white five-pointed stars fit into a circle with a diameter of 6 centimeters. The distance between the circles is 6 centimeters. The stars are arranged longitudinally and vertically in the following order: three stars in the upper row, four stars in the middle row, and five stars in the lower row. The stars in the lower row are located 3.5 centimeters from the lower end of the new moon.

State emblem of the Republic of Uzbekistan

The State Emblem of the Republic of Uzbekistan is the official state symbol of the Republic of Uzbekistan. Adopted on July 2, 1992, the State Emblem is one of the symbols of state independence.

The note

The state emblem has a round shape, on the left side of which is depicted the symbol of our food-ears of wheat, and on the right side-a priceless treasure that brings the glory of the Uzbek people to the world-cotton branches with open bowls. At the same time, this means that Uzbekistan is a sunny country. At the top of the coat of arms is an octagonal star as a symbol of freedom of Uzbekistan, inside it is a crescent and a star, meaning that the majority of the population is Muslim, and in the center is a Humo bird with wings as a symbol. happiness and freedom. At the bottom of the coat of arms on the ribbon depicting the national flag of the Republic of Uzbekistan, the word "Uzbekistan"is inscribed.
